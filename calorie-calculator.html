Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about calorie calculation

How do I calculate my daily calorie needs? ▼
Daily calorie needs are calculated using your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) multiplied by an activity factor. We use the Mifflin-St Jeor formula which multiplies your activity level: Sedentary (1.2), Light (1.375), Moderate (1.55), Active (1.725), or Very Active (1.9).
What is the difference between BMR and TDEE? ▼
BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is calories burned at rest. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is your total calorie burn including all activities and exercise. TDEE = BMR x Activity Factor.
How many calories should I eat to lose weight? ▼
Create a calorie deficit of 500 calories below your TDEE for healthy weight loss (1 pound/week). This calculator subtracts 500 for weight loss goals and adds 500 for weight gain goals.
What are macros and why do they matter? ▼
Macros are Protein, Carbs, and Fats. Protein builds muscle, Carbs provide energy, Fats support hormones. Aim for 0.7-1g protein per lb body weight for muscle building.
Is this calculator accurate? ▼
Yes! We use the scientifically validated Mifflin-St Jeor equation considered the most accurate BMR formula. Results are typically accurate within ±10%.
