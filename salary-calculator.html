Salary Calculator Guide

ToolPond Pro's salary calculator helps you understand your true take-home pay. Calculate how much you'll actually receive after federal income tax, state income tax, Social Security, Medicare, and other deductions like 401k contributions.

What is Gross vs Net Salary?

Gross Salary: Your total earnings before any deductions. Net Salary: What you actually receive after taxes and deductions. For example, a $60,000 gross salary might result in $45,000-$48,000 net depending on your location and deductions.

How to Use This Calculator

  • Gross to Net Tab: Enter your gross salary, filing status, state, and 401k to see your exact take-home pay with tax breakdown.
  • Hourly/Annual Tab: Convert between hourly wage and annual salary. Perfect for comparing job offers.
  • Overtime Tab: Calculate overtime earnings at 1.5x rate and see impact of bonuses on total compensation.

Understanding Tax Deductions

  • Federal Income Tax: Progressive tax from 10% to 37% based on income brackets
  • State Income Tax: Varies from 0% to 13% depending on state
  • Social Security: 6.2% of wages up to annual wage base
  • Medicare: 1.45% of all wages
  • 401k Contributions: Pre-tax retirement savings (reduces taxable income)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a salary calculator? +

A salary calculator converts between different pay formats (hourly, weekly, monthly, annual), calculates take-home pay after taxes and deductions, and helps you understand your true compensation.

How do I calculate my annual salary from hourly wage? +

Multiply your hourly wage by hours per week, then multiply by 52 weeks. Example: $25/hour × 40 hours × 52 weeks = $52,000 annual. Our calculator does this instantly.

What is the difference between gross and net salary? +

Gross salary is your total earnings before any deductions. Net salary (take-home pay) is what you actually receive after federal income tax, state income tax, Social Security, Medicare, and other deductions are subtracted.

How much tax is deducted from my salary? +

Tax deductions typically include federal income tax (10-37% based on brackets), state income tax (0-13%), Social Security (6.2%), and Medicare (1.45%). Our calculator estimates all these based on 2026 tax rates.

Is this calculator accurate? +

Yes! Our calculator uses current 2026 federal tax brackets. For precise figures, consult a tax professional, but our estimates are excellent for planning and comparison purposes.
