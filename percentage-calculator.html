Common Percentage Calculations

Here are some of the most common percentage calculations people use every day:

  • 10% of a number: Move the decimal point one place to the left. 10% of 250 = 25.
  • 20% tip: Divide the bill by 5. 20% of $80 = $16.
  • 50% off: Divide the price by 2. 50% of $100 = $50.
  • 25% discount: Divide the price by 4. 25% of $200 = $50.
  • 5% sales tax: Divide the price by 20. 5% of $100 = $5.
  • Percentage increase: ((New - Old) ÷ Old) × 100. Salary from $50k to $55k = 10% increase.
  • Percentage decrease: ((Old - New) ÷ Old) × 100. Price from $100 to $80 = 20% decrease.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a percentage?

A percentage is a number expressed as a fraction of 100. For example, 50% means 50 out of 100, or half of a total amount. The symbol "%" is used to denote percentages.

How do you calculate a percentage of a number?

To calculate a percentage of a number, multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. Formula: (Number × Percentage) ÷ 100 = Result. For example, 20% of 150 = (150 × 20) ÷ 100 = 30.

How to calculate percentage increase?

Subtract the original value from the new value, divide by the original value, then multiply by 100. Formula: ((New - Original) ÷ Original) × 100. For example, if your salary increased from $50,000 to $55,000, the increase is ((55000 - 50000) ÷ 50000) × 100 = 10%.

How to calculate percentage decrease?

Subtract the new value from the original value, divide by the original value, then multiply by 100. Formula: ((Original - New) ÷ Original) × 100. For example, if a product price dropped from $100 to $80, the decrease is ((100 - 80) ÷ 100) × 100 = 20%.

How to calculate discount percentage?

Subtract the sale price from the original price, divide by the original price, then multiply by 100. Formula: ((Original - Sale) ÷ Original) × 100. For example, a $100 item on sale for $75 has a discount of ((100 - 75) ÷ 100) × 100 = 25%.
