How Age is Calculated

Our age calculator uses a precise algorithm to determine your exact age from your date of birth. Here's how it works:

  • Years: Subtract your birth year from the current year. If your birthday hasn't occurred yet this year, subtract one year.
  • Months: Calculate the difference between the current month and your birth month. If the current day is before your birth day, subtract one month.
  • Days: Count the exact days from your last birthday to today, accounting for different month lengths (28, 29, 30, or 31 days).
  • Total Days: Calculate the total number of days from your birth date to today, including leap years.

This method ensures your chronological age is calculated with complete accuracy, accounting for all calendar variations including leap years.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How do I calculate my exact age?

Simply enter your date of birth in our free age calculator. It will instantly show your exact age in years, months, days, and the total number of days you have lived. No manual calculation or signup is required.

How many days until my next birthday?

Our age calculator automatically calculates and displays the number of days remaining until your next birthday. This countdown updates in real-time based on the current date.

Can I use this age calculator for official forms?

Yes, our age calculator provides accurate chronological age calculations suitable for official forms, eligibility checks, school admissions, job applications, and legal purposes. The calculation accounts for leap years and exact calendar dates.

How is age calculated from date of birth?

Age is calculated by subtracting the birth year from the current year, then adjusting for months and days. If the current month is before the birth month, or the same month but an earlier day, one year is subtracted. Months and days are calculated precisely, accounting for varying month lengths and leap years.

Does the calculator account for leap years?

Yes, our age calculator fully accounts for leap years (February 29) in its calculations. If you were born on a leap day, the calculator handles your birthday correctly, treating February 28 or March 1 as your birthday in non-leap years depending on the calculation context.
