Understanding Macronutrients

🥩 Protein (4 calories per gram)

Protein is essential for muscle building, tissue repair, enzyme production, and immune function. High protein diets support weight loss by increasing satiety and preserving muscle mass. Good sources include chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, tofu, and legumes.

🍞 Carbohydrates (4 calories per gram)

Carbs are the body's primary energy source, especially for high-intensity exercise and brain function. Complex carbs (whole grains, vegetables, fruits) provide sustained energy and fiber. Simple carbs (sugar, white bread) spike blood sugar quickly.

🥑 Fats (9 calories per gram)

Dietary fats are crucial for hormone production (including testosterone), brain function, nutrient absorption, and cell membrane health. Focus on healthy fats from avocados, nuts, olive oil, and fatty fish. Limit trans fats and excessive saturated fats.

Popular Diet Types & Macro Ratios

Balanced

🥗 Balanced Diet (30% Protein / 40% Carbs / 30% Fats)

The most sustainable approach for general health and moderate activity levels. Provides adequate energy, supports muscle maintenance, and includes enough healthy fats for hormone health. Perfect for beginners and those who want flexibility.

High Protein

💪 High Protein Diet (40% Protein / 40% Carbs / 20% Fats)

Ideal for bodybuilders, strength athletes, and those in a muscle-building phase. The higher protein intake maximizes muscle protein synthesis, while moderate carbs fuel intense workouts. Lower fats help control total calories.

Low Carb

📉 Low Carb Diet (30% Protein / 25% Carbs / 45% Fats)

Effective for weight loss and metabolic health. Reduced carbs lower insulin levels, which can help the body burn fat for fuel. Higher fats keep you satiated. Popular among those with insulin resistance or type 2 diabetes.

Keto

⚡ Ketogenic Diet (20% Protein / 10% Carbs / 70% Fats)

Induces ketosis, where the body burns fat for fuel instead of carbs. Very low carb intake (typically under 50g/day) forces the liver to produce ketones. Popular for rapid weight loss, mental clarity, and certain medical conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How do I calculate my macros?

Enter your daily calorie target, select your diet type (balanced, high protein, low carb, or keto), and choose your goal. The calculator instantly shows your daily protein, carbs, and fat targets in grams. Adjust based on your body's response over 2-4 weeks.

What are macros (macronutrients)?

Macros are the three main nutrients your body needs in large amounts: protein (4 cal/g), carbohydrates (4 cal/g), and fats (9 cal/g). Each serves different functions — protein builds muscle, carbs provide energy, and fats support hormones and brain health.

What is the best macro ratio for weight loss?

For weight loss, high protein (40P/40C/20F) or low carb (30P/25C/45F) diets often work best. Higher protein preserves muscle mass and increases satiety. Lower carbs can help control appetite and insulin levels. The best ratio is one you can stick to consistently.

How do I calculate my TDEE for macros?

Use a TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) calculator that considers your age, weight, height, gender, and activity level. This gives you your maintenance calories. For weight loss, subtract 300-500 calories. For weight gain, add 300-500 calories.

What if I don't hit my macros exactly every day?

Perfection isn't necessary — consistency matters more. Aim to be within 5-10% of your macro targets daily. Weekly averages are more important than daily precision. Focus on hitting protein first, then carbs and fats can be more flexible.
