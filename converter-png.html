Frequently Asked Questions

What is PJP format and why should I convert it to another format? ▼

PJP is a progressive JPEG file format commonly found on older devices or specific systems. While PJP offers reasonable compression, JPG, PNG, WebP, and other modern formats offer better compatibility, superior compression, and additional features. Converting PJP to a more widely supported format ensures your images work seamlessly across all devices, browsers, and applications. Modern formats like WebP offer better compression without quality loss, while PNG provides transparency support for design work.

Which quality settings should I use for the best results? ▼

The ideal quality setting depends on your specific use case. For web images and social media sharing, 75-85% quality offers an excellent balance of visual quality and reasonable file size. For professional photography or print preparation, use 90-100% quality to maintain maximum detail. For thumbnails, icons, or images where quality is less critical, 40-60% is acceptable and creates significantly smaller files. Experiment with different settings to find the perfect balance for your particular needs.

What is the difference between JPG, PNG, and WebP formats? ▼

JPG is a lossy compression format best for photographs and complex images. PNG is a lossless format that supports transparency and is ideal for graphics and design. WebP is a modern format offering superior compression compared to both JPG and PNG while supporting both lossy and lossless compression. Choose JPG for photographs, PNG for images requiring transparency or crisp graphics, and WebP for optimized web performance with the best compression ratios available.
