Frequently Asked Questions About QR Codes

What is a QR code and how does it work?
A QR code (Quick Response code) is a two-dimensional barcode that stores information in a pattern of black and white squares. When scanned with a smartphone camera or QR code reader, it instantly decodes the stored information. QR codes can store URLs, text, contact information, WiFi credentials, and more. They're read from any angle and work offline, making them incredibly versatile.

What data types can I encode in a QR code?
Our QR code generator supports seven different data types: URLs/website links, plain text, vCard (contact information), WiFi network credentials, SMS messages, email addresses, and phone numbers. Select the appropriate type before generating your code to ensure the data is encoded correctly for the intended use.
Should I download as PNG or SVG?
Choose PNG for digital use (websites, social media, emails) as it's universally compatible and displays perfectly on all devices. Choose SVG for printing and professional materials because it scales to any size without losing quality. SVG is also ideal for documents that will be printed at various sizes.
Is my data stored or tracked when I create a QR code?
No. Your data is never stored, saved, or shared. All QR code generation happens locally in your browser without any server connection. Your privacy is completely protected - we don't track, log, or analyze your QR codes. This makes our tool safe for generating codes with sensitive or proprietary information.
How accurate is the error correction feature?
QR code error correction allows the code to be scanned even if partially damaged or obscured. We offer four levels: Low (7% recovery), Medium (15% recovery), Quartile (25% recovery), and High (30% recovery). Medium is suitable for most uses. Use High error correction for codes that might be exposed to damage or weather.
Can I use these QR codes for commercial purposes?
Yes! You can use QR codes generated by our tool for any purpose - personal, business, marketing, education, or commercial applications. There are no restrictions on use. The QR codes work for retail, restaurants, event tickets, business cards, marketing campaigns, packaging, and any other commercial or personal purpose you can imagine.
Does the QR code need internet to be scanned?
The QR code itself doesn't require internet to be scanned - it's just an image. However, the data it contains might require internet. For example, a URL QR code needs internet to access the website, while a WiFi QR code helps connect to a network. Text, contact, or SMS QR codes work without internet once decoded by your phone.
