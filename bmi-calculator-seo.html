Understanding BMI & Health

What Does BMI Mean?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a numerical measure of body fat based on height and weight. It's a screening tool used by healthcare professionals worldwide to identify potential weight-related health issues.

BMI Categories Explained

Underweight (BMI < 18.5): May indicate insufficient body weight. Normal Weight (BMI 18.5-24.9): Healthy weight range for most adults. Overweight (BMI 25-29.9): Above healthy weight range. Obese (BMI ≥ 30): Significantly above healthy range, may increase health risks.

Is BMI Accurate?

BMI is a useful screening tool for most people but doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat. Athletes may have high BMI despite low body fat. Always consult healthcare providers for personalized health assessment.

Healthy BMI Range

For most adults, a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy weight. However, ideal BMI varies by age, gender, and individual factors. Consult healthcare professionals for personalized recommendations.

FAQ

How does the BMI calculator work?

Enter your height in centimeters and weight in kilograms. The calculator uses the formula: BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)². Results appear instantly with your BMI number and health category.

Is the BMI calculator free?

Yes, completely free forever with unlimited BMI calculations and no signup required.

What is a normal BMI?

A normal BMI ranges from 18.5 to 24.9. This range is generally considered healthy for most adults.

Is BMI accurate for everyone?

BMI is a useful screening tool for most people but doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or age. Athletes and older adults may need personalized health assessment.

Should I track BMI regularly?

Yes, tracking BMI monthly can help monitor weight changes and fitness progress. However, don't obsess over daily fluctuations due to water weight and other factors.
